Safety

You probably already know that Rio isn't the safest city in the world, but it is also not as bad as it is sometimes portrayed.

Here are my tipps to avoid trouble.

Ah and just for the record, you live in one of the safest areas of Rio...

The community (it is actually not appropriate to say favela as it is derogatory) of Vidigal is in a unique situation, where the police is actually in control, and collaborates with the ruling gang (if you want to learn more about this, I am happy to elaborate), so nothing I write below applies here.

You can walk around the area here completely safe at any time of the day or night, with your phone, jewelry etc, nothing will happen to you.

This also applies to Vidigal Beach.

When it comes to Leblon, Ipanema, Copacabana and especially Centro, you have to behave vastly different:

  • Don't use any kind of expensive looking jewelry/watches.
  • Keep your phone in your pocket unless you are aware of your surroundings (it is fine to use on the beach etc to take photos but be aware of your surroundings, and don't walk around mindlessly with phone in the hand).
  • Backup your phone data, or even better leave it at home or bring a cheaper phone to use on the street.
  • If you are unlucky and fall victim to an assault/robbery attempt, do not resist. Give up your phone/belongings. They will want you to unlock your phone too. So best to not have bank account apps installed.
  • Please, this is really important. Anytime something bad happens it is because the victim tries to fight back/keep the phone.
  • These people have nothing to lose and will shoot you or stab you without hesitation if you do not cooperate.
  • However, if you stay in the tourist areas, you will be fine... Risk for petty theft is much higher, so just be smart about your surroundings.

If you want to go somewhere, you can tell me and I will offer advice. Eg, Arpoador is an amazing sunset spot (your apartment is the best sunrise spot – I really recommend to wake up at 6 one day and enjoy it!). There is also a street market, often live music, free yoga in the morning – a magical spot. But it should be avoided on weekends at around 4 pm to 7 pm, because it gets very crowded with youths from bad districts...

When you go out, don't bring anyone home (it is also not allowed/possible to bring unregistered visitors back to the apartment – for safety reasons). There are Motels all around where people meet for casual encounters – much safer.

Always watch your drink. When you go to Lapa , don't wander around the side streets.

Generally, it is always good to stay where there are many people, don't walk around empty streets (especially in the center / Copacabana / Leblon / Ipanema is less risky).

When you are out and about at night take taxis / Uber even for short distances...

Again, none of that applies to where you live and the surrounding area, there you can be completely relaxed. No robberies, no assaults, no thefts will happen here.

Any questions, let me know.

Vidigal

Vidigal is a small peaceful pacified community on the hill of Morro Dois Irmãos , with amazing views of Rio de Janeiro.

It is the first community that became a hot tourist destination, not least because of the famous hike in a national park up to the top of the hill. Full hike guide →

It is a relatively easy hike of about 30–40 minutes if you start at the official entrance point.

To go there you can take a moto-taxi or minivan (≈ R$ 10 per person).

You can also walk up from the bottom at Praça do Vidigal , which is considerably harder, doubling the time to the top of the mountain.

Personally, I prefer walking it this way—the path is easy to find (just follow the one main road), and when in doubt ask one of the friendly locals.

Entrance fee to the hike is R$ 10 per person.

It is really worth starting the hike when it is still dark so you can enjoy the most spectacular sunrise of your life.

The views from up there are truly stunning at any time—it is the best viewpoint in Rio.

Best time to visit is during the week, as on weekends it sometimes gets crowded.

On the way down, grab a snack (coxinha—a fried doughball filled with chicken) and an açaí, then go straight to Vidigal Beach , where you can wash away the sweat with a dip in the ocean.

It is a very safe beach and still something of a secret (though that’s changing fast), very different from the buzzing shores of Ipanema or Copacabana.

Other things to do in Vidigal:

  • An amazing breakfast spot called Café e Mar . Check their Instagram and reserve a table in advance.
  • Bar da Laje is a famous rooftop viewpoint serving food and drinks (cover ≈ R$ 80 pp).
  • Mirante do Arvrão is another popular spot for live music, DJs and events.

Beaches

Rio has plenty of beaches to enjoy. They are amazing and a huge part of the Carioca culture.

Here I will give you a brief summary of the best ones in the area, and which ones serve best for what activities ( sport, relaxation, party… )

Just a word on water quality, Almost all of Rio´s beaches have very good water quality and are safe to enter most of the times. Even beaches in the lagoon near the city center, like Botafogo, Flamengo Beach who have long been plagued with pollution, are now increasingly getting better. Personally, I would not enter the water in Botafogo, but the Flamengo beach surely is worth a visit, not least to see the numerous sea turtles living there.

To verify the water quality, and whether it is safe to enter the water, see the website Praia Limpa.

Note: At times you can see some sort of yellowish / white foam on the water surface. This does not mean that the water is dirty. It is algae growth that is not harmful to humans and even indicative of a productive ocean ecosystem (NOAA Sea Foam Facts).

When you go to the beach, you’ll be approached to rent a sun umbrella and beach chairs, which, in my opinion, is nice to have.

Prices vary, but a reputable place will have a table of prices.

It will be more expensive in summer or on weekends but should not exceed 15 to 35 for an umbrella and about 15 for a chair.

You are free to use them as long as you want.

VIDIGAL BEACH

This is the beach closest to your home, just a few minute walk away. It is my favourite beach in Rio de Janeiro because of its quiet and secluded nature.

It is almost exclusively used by Vidigal residents and visitors and the Hotel Sheraton, and does not have a lot of beach vendors, which means it is perfect to relax.

Just like in the Vidigal community / area, crime is not tolerated, so it is one of the few places in Rio where you can relax and don’t have to watch your stuff like a hawk while you enter the water (always good to exercise some sort of caution though, especially in summer when it gets crowded).

The best time to go is right at or before sunrise (around 6 am) until midday, when the sun will disappear there due to the Dois Irmaos hill behind.

Just one word of caution, that at times when the waves are high, the beach literally disappears, or becomes very small. Just have a look from the veranda of your penthouse, or a good rule of thumb is to not go when the stairs that lead to the beach are under water.

You do not have to worry though, the beach has a life guard, who will put flags into the sand which signal whether or not it is safe on that day.

LEBLON BEACH

The second nearest beach, and also a personal favorite due to its relaxing atmosphere (compared to Ipanema or Copacabana), is Leblon beach, just 13 minute sea stroll away. The best place is in between Posto 11 and Posto 12.

IPANEMA BEACH

If you want more action, Ipanema is your place to go. There will be a lot of beach vendors, so you can sit all day in the same place, getting served all sorts of drinks and foods.

Around Posto 10 is known for its various sportive offerings. Posto 9 is a place to see and be seen, whereas non-stop (wild!) LGBT party atmosphere is found by going further down in direction Copacabana, just look out for the respective rainbow flags.

At the very end of Ipanema beach is Arpoador, a famous sunset spot, and also the best for surfing in the south zone. There is also a hippy market on Sundays, and generally a lot going on. Free yoga at 7 am, beach tennis, surfers, food. It is a great place to hangout.

A word of caution though, while there is a police officer present at pretty much all times, it can get rowdy / dangerous on hot summer (weekend) days around 3 to 6 pm. Reason is that nearby the buses from the north zone have their end point, which means you will have hordes of young often drunk people, and it is better to avoid going there as a tourist.

Also, on the Arpoador rock there are sometimes criminals trying to take advantage of unsuspecting tourists. If you go there (and you should, it is definitely an amazing vibe), just don’t go off the beaten path and stick to where the crowds are.

COPACABANA BEACH

To be quite honest, while there are nice spots too, there is no real reason for you to go to Copa beach. It is very touristy, so it attracts a lot of criminals trying to take advantage and it does not offer anything special. Notable exception is Standup paddling in the area around Fairmont Hotel. Very nice, especially during sunrise, but also very crowded. Personally I think nothing beats being alone at Vidigal Beach for sunrise.

SAO CONRADO

Another beach nearby. The nicest part is the walk there though; between around 5 pm and 6 pm, the sky shows its magnificent colours while you stroll along the coast.

Warning: When the waves are very high, the path is closed, as announced via speakers and an alarm sound.

Sao Conrado itself is a great place to body-board, but for simply relaxing and swimming, the water is a bit too wild, in my opinion. Also, the good spots can get crowded with people from the nearby Rocinha community.

At the end of the beach is the landing spot for the paragliders from “Pedra Bonita.” I also heard that the beach bar named Musa has a good burger though.

BARRA BEACH

Barra is a relatively recently developed district, often called the Miami of Rio de Janeiro. It has an extremely long stretch of beach, which means you can find really quiet places there.

There you also find upscale beach clubs, where you can hang out to music and drinks while watching the new rich of Rio. A notable one would be K08 Kite Surf Club. The burger there is terrible though.

If you want to go jet skiing, go to Praia dos Amores.
